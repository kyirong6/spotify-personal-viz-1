Personal Spotify Viz!

This is a series of visualizations that aid in exploring and analyzing my listening habits. The data was fetched using spotipy, a Python client for the Spotify Web API.

The data behind these visualizations include my top artists, genres, and audio features I gravitate towards. Moreover, the data is also divided into different time ranges. The short_term uses the past 4 weeks of data, medium_term is the past 6 months, and long_term is several years worth of data.

Audio features describe various attributes of a song such as: danceability, energy, speechiness, valence, and more...Analyzing and visualizing these features allows me to better understand the types of songs I listen to and why I listen to them. Moreover, viewing my listening habits over a time range also gives me some feedback on how my tendancies have changed over time.

Average of Audio Features on All Time Ranges

The bar chart to the left describes my average audio features in totality. Spotify describes each feature as such:

  1. danceability: "Danceability describes how suitable a track is for dancing based on a combination elements including tempo, rhythm stability, and beat."
  2. energy: "A perceptual measure of intensity and activity. Energetic tracks feel fast, loud, and noisy."
  3. speechiness: "Speechiness detects the presence of spoken words in a track. The more exclusively speech-like the recording (e.g. talk show), the closer to 1.0 the attribute value."
  4. acousticness: "A confidence measure from 0.0 to 1.0 of whether the track is acoustic. 1.0 represents high confidence the track is acoustic."
  5. instrumentalness: "Predicts whether a track contains no vocals."
  6. liveness: "Detects the presence of an audience in the recording."
  7. valence: "A measure from 0.0 to 1.0 describing the musical positiveness conveyed by a track."
